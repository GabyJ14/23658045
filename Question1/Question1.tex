\section{\texorpdfstring{Introduction
\label{Introduction}}{Introduction }}\label{introduction}

The US Baby Names dataset is analysed in this report. Exploratory
analysis is firstly done to see what is in the data. A time-series
analysis is then done followed by an Influencer analysis.

\#Exploratory Data Analysis In this section I examine the overall trends
in baby names over the years.

\includegraphics{Question1_files/figure-latex/unnamed-chunk-2-1.pdf} The
figure above shows that Mary was the most popular name across history
for girls.

\includegraphics{Question1_files/figure-latex/unnamed-chunk-3-1.pdf} The
figure above that shows the top 10 names for boys over history show that
James is the most popular. John and Robert is close 2nd and 3rd now. It
is interesting that the top names for boys are more closer to each other
than for girls.

\#Time-Series Analysis In this section the Spearman rank correlation of
the top 25 baby names from each year with the following three years.
This rank correlation is then used to analyse the persistence of name
popularity over decades, especially focussing on 1990's and onwards.
